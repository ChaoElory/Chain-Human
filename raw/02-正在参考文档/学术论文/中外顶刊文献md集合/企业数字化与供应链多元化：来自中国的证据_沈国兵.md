内容提要：在数字经济快速发展的背景下，企业数字化如何赋能供应链多元
化、提升韧性，变得尤为重要。本文构建理论模型探究风险规避型企业如何在信
息摩擦下配置供应商结构，揭示出企业数字化促进供应链多元化的微观机制。基

（1）企业数字化显著提升了其供应链多元
化水平，在使用倾向得分加权法、外生政策冲击和工具变量之后依然稳健；
（2）企业
数字化对供应链多元化的促进作用对于数字经济发展水平较低地区的企业和非
制造业企业的影响更大；
（3）企业数字化通过提升信息搜寻能力、创新能力和议价
能力间接促进了供应链多元化。据此，大力发展数字经济可推动企业供应链多元
化，提高企业供应链韧性。

本文针对企业数字化促进产业链供应链多元化，在两个方面做出探究：第一，理论模
型上，建立了一个风险规避型企业在信息摩擦下的供应商选择模型，探究了企业数字化影
响供应链多元化的内在机制。企业通过提高供应链多元化水平来降低风险，但是企业与
供应商之间存在信息摩擦，扩展供应商范围需要一定的信息成本。企业数字化可以降低
信息摩擦成本，进而促进企业供应链多元化。第二，经验分析上，本文使用中国上市公司
数据，创新性地基于企业披露的前五大供应商信息，利用幂律规则（power law）反推估算出
企业供应商总数，构建出更贴近理论定义的企业供应链多元化指标。据此，检验了企业数
字化对中国企业供应链多元化的影响，并对其异质性效应和机制渠道进行了分析。
与本文相关的文献主要有以下三支：第一，有关企业数字化效应的研究。数字经济已
逐渐成为经济增长的新动力。赵涛等（2020）（2019）认为，数字经济可以提升宏观经济的
高质量发展水平和生产率。部分文献探究了数字化在微观层面是如何发挥作用的。Allen
（2014）提出数字化降低了信息壁垒，可以帮助企业搜寻到更多目标市场，增进整体福利。
信息畅通还改变了企业生产率，Bartel et al.（2007）发现使用信息技术提升了企业生产率，
因为企业内部信息畅通增强了企业的管理效率（沈国兵和袁征宇，2020），同时企业和外部
的知识交流促进了创新活动（Shen et al.，2023）。而且，部分文献探究了数字化对企业绩效
的影响，如股票流动性（吴非等，2021）、分工专业化（袁淳等，2021）等。不过，尚未有文献
分析企业数字化如何影响企业的供应链决策，特别是企业供应链多元化的决策。
第二，有关产业链供应链风险及多元化的研究。在一个包含上下游的生产网络中，供
应链上某一环节的生产率冲击会沿着链条向外传播，由此导致了风险扩散（Acemoglu et
al.，2012）。当上游供应商因自然灾害等因素受到负面冲击后，下游企业也会随之受损
（Carvalho et al.，2021）。当上下游关系的可替代性较低时，冲击的传播效应会更强（Barrot
& Sauvagnat，2016）。为了应对供应链中断风险，供应链多元化是重要的措施之一。在宏
观层面，Caselli et al.（2020）发现供应链多元化显著降低了整体经济的波动性。在微观层
面，Gervais（2018）证实供应链风险增加会促使企业拓展供应链多元化。此外，信息在供应
链风险的传播上发挥着重要作用。La’o & Tahbaz-Salehi（2022）发现，信息摩擦导致的价
格黏性会影响供应链上的冲击传播和货币政策效果。
第三，有关企业数字化在供应链多元化中作用的研究。数字化通过影响企业特征、交
易成本、信息摩擦等途径影响企业供应链多元化。从企业特征视角，Federgruen & Yang
（2009）采用一系列模型探究了企业存货、需求端和供给端风险对供应链多元化的影响。
Huang（2017）通过异质性企业模型发现生产率更高的企业更倾向于建立多元化的供应链。
从交易成本视角，Gervais（2018）发现关税和非关税贸易成本下降促进了进口供应链多元

化指标。第五，深化了数字经济应用的政策含义。本文发现，企业数字化对供应链多元化
的促进效应对于数字经济发展水平较低地区的企业和非制造业企业的影响更显著，而对

和深化制造业产业数字化转型，有利于提升整体经济韧性。

## 第 22 页

沈国兵、沈彬朝：企业数字化与供应链多元化：来自中国的证据
Firm Digitalization and Supply Chain Diversification:
Evidence from China
SHEN Guobing
a,b and SHEN Binchao
b
(a: Institute of World Economy, Fudan University;
b: School of Economics, Fudan University)
Summary: In recent years, factors like the escalation of U.S.-China trade frictions and global public health
crises have continuously disrupted the stability of global industrial and supply chains, highlighting the ur‐
gency of enhancing their resilience and security. Meanwhile, China’s digital economy has developed rapidly,
with the scale of industrial digitalization expanding steadily, injecting new momentum into the transformation
and upgrading of the real economy. Against this backdrop, exploring how firm digitalization shapes supply
chain diversification holds significant theoretical and practical value. Theoretically, existing literature mostly
focuses on the impact of digitalization on firm productivity or innovation, with few in-depth analyses of its
role in reshaping supply chain configuration decisions and a lack of theoretical frameworks integrating infor‐
mation frictions and risk-averse behaviors. Practically, as China actively promotes supply chain diversifica‐
tion and the deep integration of the digital and real economies, clarifying effects of firm digitalization on sup‐
ply chain diversification and its mechanisms can provide a basis for the government to formulate targeted
digital transformation policies and for firms to optimize supply chain risk management, offering important in‐
sights for building an independent, controllable, safe, and reliable modern industrial system.
This paper systematically studies the impact of firm digitalization on supply chain diversification and its
mechanisms by constructing a theoretical model and conducting empirical tests with data from Chinese listed
companies covering the years from 2000 to 2022. Theoretically, it establishes a supplier selection model for
risk-averse firms under information frictions, revealing three micro-channels through which digitalization pro‐
motes supply chain diversification: reducing information search costs, enhancing innovation capacity, and
strengthening bargaining power. Digitization eases information asymmetry between firms and potential sup‐
pliers, encouraging firms to expand supplier networks for risk diversification. Empirically, this study innova‐
tively estimates the unobserved total supplier numbers by extrapolating from top-five supplier data using the
power law rule, constructing a supply chain diversification indicator aligned with theoretical definitions.
Baseline regression shows that digitalization significantly improves supply chain diversification, a conclusion
robust to propensity score weighting addressing self-selection bias, the “Broadband China” policy as an exog‐
enous shock, and instrumental variable methods. Heterogeneity analysis indicates the effect is more pro‐
nounced for firms in less digitally developed regions and non-manufacturing sectors. Mechanism tests adopt‐
ing Heckman et al.’s (2013) framework confirm the existence of the information, innovation, and bargaining
channels.
The paper’s marginal contributions are as follows. First, we expand the theoretical model of supply
chain diversification to incorporate information frictions. Second, we improve mechanism identification
methods to mitigate endogeneity. Third, we propose a direct measure of information frictions based on sup‐
plier information disclosure. Fourth, different from the common practice in literature of using the proportion
of top five suppliers, we propose a power law-based supply chain diversification indicator, which more
closely aligns with the essence of supply chain diversification. Finally, we provide policy implications for
how the digital economy enhances industrial chain resilience with evidence from Chinese firms.
Based on the findings, we propose the following policy recommendations. First, efforts should be made
to strengthen digital infrastructure (e.g., 5G, data centers) and support firms in adopting supply chain digital
technologies, using funding and tax incentives to encourage system upgrades and R&D, while launching pilot
projects to reduce risks. Second, it is crucial to optimize regional allocation of digital resources, prioritizing
support for digitally lagging regions, manufacturing firms, and SOEs in digital transformation through talent
development and evaluation mechanisms. Third, it is necessary to build digital supply chain platforms to fa‐
cilitate data sharing and inclusive services, promoting collaborative innovation and bulk purchasing to en‐
hance chain resilience.
Keywords: Firm Digitalization; Supply Chain Diversification; Information Frictions; Supply Chain Resil‐
ience
